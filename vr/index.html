Why are we doing this?

In Virginia, college students are eligible to register to vote, even if they come from another state

As long as they are:

  • US citizens
  • 18 years old on Election Day (November 4th, 2025)
  • not currently declared "mentally incompetent" by a court
  • not disenfranchised by a felony conviction
    • note: if you have questions about restoring voting rights, email Prof. Singerman (ds2ax@)

In 2025, Virginians will elect executive offices + 100 out of 140 seats in the legislature

But college students vote at way lower rates than everyone else

Why register to vote here instead of at home?

  • UVA & the Cville area is your home for the next 4 years
  • Virginia is a swing-y state
  • Local and state government decisions affect your daily life a lot
  • It's easier to vote here than to figure out how to get home for Election Day
  • It's more fun to vote in person than to mail an envelope

2. Voting info

FAQ

What is the registration deadline?

October 24. We are collecting applications now to make sure they are all processed in time.

Can I register online?

Yes, if you have a Virginia driver's license or state ID card. But you will forget.

Where do I vote on Election Day?

Slaughter Rec Center, 6am-7pm.

What ID do I need?

Your UVA ID works (plastic OR electronic). Your out-of-state drivers license does not.

Can I vote early in person?

Yes: after your registration is processed, you can vote at the County Office Building, 1600 5th Street Extended (you need a car). M-F 8:30am-5pm.

Can I vote by mail?

Yes: after your registration is processed, go to elections.virginia.gov to request a ballot.

Can I register on election day?

Yes but you will need to cast a special provisional ballot.

Does registering to vote change other official addresses?

No, your DMV address and other addresses aren't affected.

Do I need to register in Charlottesville even if I'm already registered elsewhere in VA?

Yes, if you want to vote here you need to register here.
